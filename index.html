About

The PM who reads the Snowflake query and talks to the merchant.

I'm Mariana — a product manager with five years at Teya, a European payments company, where I've owned everything from terminal hardware UX to payments infrastructure scaling to self-initiated programmes that weren't on anyone's roadmap.

I studied PPE at Oxford, which taught me to read a situation carefully and argue a position clearly. That shows up in how I write product specs, how I run discovery, and how I get cross-functional alignment on decisions that aren't obvious.

I'm Brazilian, based in London, and looking for my next role. I'm open to any strong product organisation: fintech, consumer, B2B, e-commerce. What matters to me is the quality of the problem and the calibre of the team.

What I'm good at

Finding problems no one asked about. The $12m inventory recovery, the tipping A/B test, the in-terminal notification system — none of these were on the roadmap. I initiated them because the data pointed somewhere and I followed it.
Making complex systems feel simple. Payments infrastructure, PCI compliance, multi-scheme card processing — my job is to absorb that complexity and give merchants a product that feels obvious. The terminal redesign reduced support tickets by 85% on some categories.
Getting things shipped across markets. I've launched products simultaneously across 7 European countries, coordinating engineering, legal, compliance, localisation, and customer relations. I know what multi-market delivery actually costs.
Earning CPO and CTO sponsorship. The MOTO launch required cross-functional resources I didn't control. I built the case, secured executive sponsorship, and delivered in 6 months across 6 markets.
